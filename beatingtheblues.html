Beating The Winter Blues With Yoga

By Charlotte posted March 25, 2018

How yoga can help us feel fresh and energised during the grey winter (or spring or autumn) days (including some home sequencing advice).

Alright we've all been there (at least over here in the UK): having to survive again and again long periods without sunshine and a grey sky hovering above us. I still cannot believe that I haven't followed my urge of moving south- it remains a sunny dream so far. From my experience, yoga and meditation can help me get through the grey months big time. A good home practice is essential for me since it can help structure my day without much effort.

Home Routine

For example, just taking some time for introspection in the morning or evening (or whenever you have time, really) to sit still and tuning into yourself can help you build a healthy routine and feel more centred. Having a daily mindful moving routine leads to a more energised feeling all day. And by that I don't necessarily refer to a 60 minutes asana practice; many of us simply cannot find the time each day. Merely 30, 15 or even 10 minutes per day can have a huge impact. Connecting to the breath, observing how the body feels. Are any thoughts coming up? Try to gently push them aside during your time of practice. And then maybe start to move slowly, according to how you feel today.

Maybe it's just a short set of restorative poses, some mindful breathing in child pose or a reclining baddha konasana (bound angle pose). You might feel more active and start by warming your body with a few sun salutations, whatever feels good for you. I can also very much recommend to include an inversion in your daily practice. It doesn't have to be an advanced one, any inversion is good for your blood circulation. Beginners might choose a long downward dog or forward fold (uttanasana or prasarita padottanasana); more advanced practitioners might go for a shoulder-, head- or handstand. As always in yoga practice: Mastering a basic pose well and getting all the benefits out of it is more important than mastering an advanced asana halfway without being able to e.g. breathe properly.

At the end of each routine it might feel nice to come back to that sense of introspection and take a moment to observe how your body feels now. Does your breath flow differently? Has your energy level changed? And always remember: you are not alone!! Summer will be here, I promise :)
