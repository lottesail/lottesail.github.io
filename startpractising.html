Why I Started Practising Yoga

By Charlotte posted March 20, 2018

In this first blog entry I philosophise about the yoga glow and why I never want to give up yoga.

I started yoga in 2010 at a vipasana retreat at Wat Suan Mokh in Chaya, Thailand. 10 days without speaking, 10 days filled with meditation and yoga every morning. After that I had times when I didn't practice actively but the seed was planted and in my everyday life I always came back to what I had learned: Breathing deeply, concentrating on one task at a time, good posture, taking time off for exercise and meditation...  I am not at all claiming that I master these aspects but I often use the techniques to be more aware, more mindful in my daily tasks. Which is also what yoga is about for me: Being mindful and feeling good about oneself by connecting body, breath and mind . 

Have you ever experienced that feeling after a yoga class when it seems like you're walking on clouds? I call it the yoga glow and I just love it! Yoga gave me the tool to be more satisfied with my actions and to generally feel much better about myself mentally and physically. It's a practice for everyone since it's comprised of so many different aspects. Some might come to yoga with the desire for exercise, others seek refuge from their stressful lives, a time-out. Being about moving with your breath, yoga can accommodate all these longings. Keep it up, it might as well change your lives!
